Critical Health Watchlist

As a Large Breed, GSDs have specific vulnerabilities. Prevention is key to a long life.

Joint Health (Dysplasia)

20% of GSDs face hip or elbow issues. Avoid high-impact jumping until 18 months old.

The "Silent" Danger (Bloat)

Deep-chested breeds can suffer stomach twisting. Use slow feeders and avoid exercise after meals.

German Shepherd FAQ

When is a German Shepherd "fully grown"?

Height is usually reached by 12 months, but they are not physically "mature" until 2 to 2.5 years when their chest and shoulders fill out.

Why is my puppy so "mouthy"?

GSDs are a "herding" breed, often called "Land Sharks" as puppies. They use their mouths to explore. Consistent bite-inhibition training is vital before 6 months.

How much exercise do they actually need?

An adult GSD needs 1-2 hours of daily activity. This must include mental work (scent games, advanced commands) or they may become destructive.
